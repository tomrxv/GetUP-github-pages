Faridabad's School by Solidari'Terre

16 students from EM Lyon Business School and the Ecole Centrale de Lyon challenged themselves to travel for one month in July 2018 in order to build a school in a slum in Faridabad, a city located 30 km from New Delhi, in India. This project is organized by the Emlyenne and Centralienne Solidari'terre association in partnership with Les Enfants du Shanti, a French Association of International Solidarity.

The Mission

Our mission has 3 goals:
  • To build a school in order for 25 Indian children and teenagers to have access to education
  • To bring support to the Slum School. We would accompany 54 children in their homework, assist the teachers present on the spot, take charge of a class and teach children about hygiene in order to limit the risks of contamination.
  • To bring awareness to the importance of hygiene and to participate in the daily activities of the Alice in Wonderland orphanage, which welcomes a dozen children from 6 to 15 years.

What will the funds raised be used for?

All the funds we raise will be donated to Enfants de Shanti Association and will be used for the purchase of the land (2866 €) and materials (1433 €) for the construction of the school. Furthermore, any donation over 50 € will be tax-exempted up to 66% (for example, a donation of 100 € will only cost you 33 €).

Why India?

With a population of more than 1 billion over an area equivalent to 5 times the size of France, India is the 2nd most populated country in the world after China. Although the country is experiencing economic growth, many inequalities remain:

  • 38% of the population is illiterate, 52% of which are women.
  • 9 years : the average time spent in school per capita.

Our project aims to reduce these inequalities by providing access to education for the poorest populations.

Solidari'Terre in a few words

Solidari'Terre is a humanitarian association whose goal since 1993 is to provide concrete and sustainable help to communities in need. A joint effort of EM Lyon and Ecole Centrale de Lyon, it brings together two campuses, two complementary courses, for more exchange of views, more openness and more purview. The association carries out actions of international solidarity, national actions (Telethon, Sidaction), but also helps local communities in the Lyon area (Soup Kitchen, tutoring in the city of Ecully).

Enfants de Shanti

Enfants de Shanti : it is over 300 members sent to the field since its creation in 2011, who invest in long-term development projects in order to help the poorest people. To date, their efforts are focused on India, in the cities of Jaipur and Faridabad. With a network of associations in France and around the world (Red Cross, Sun in India, Unicef), the association focuses on 3 major axes :

  • Medical help (Ghai Hospital),
  • Economic and Social help (Women empowerment project),
  • Educational projects (Slum School project).

It’s also 10,000 smiles given and received throughout their projects in India !

The essence of street art is to denounce, underline or signal the inequalities of today's world.

So Get Up, Stand Up: Get Up For Their Rights is the alliance of engaged street artists and humanitarian causes fighting for the rights, protection and education of children.

This platform aims to draw attention to a humanitarian project and artistic projects.

Anyone can propose a price for the piece of street art. The person who proposed the highest sum will be contacted after 30 days of campaign and will have the opportunity to acquire the work and to be kept informed of the actions carried out by the selected association. The money raised will then be used to produce other works and to raise public awareness of actions carried out by organizations working on behalf of children.

Pledges of donations do not bind the purchase obligation. The right of withdrawal at the end of the campaign remains possible.
